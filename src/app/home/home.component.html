What's the cause?

(food waste in Austria)

Food waste in Austria is a significant concern, with private households playing a substantial role in its magnitude. It is estimated that around 42% of the total food waste in the country originates from homes, amounting to approximately 577,000 tons of discarded food annually. Several factors contribute to this wastage.

Inadequate meal planning is a common issue, leading to excessive food preparation and subsequent disposal. Additionally, a lack of portion control results in leftovers being discarded. Misinterpretation of expiration dates also plays a role, as consumers often discard food that is still perfectly safe to consume. Impulse buying, overstocking, and a general lack of knowledge regarding proper food storage and preservation techniques further contribute to the problem.

The consequences of food waste are far-reaching. Environmentally, it leads to unnecessary greenhouse gas emissions and the wastage of precious resources such as water and land. Socially, food waste represents missed opportunities to address hunger and food insecurity within Austria.

By taking small but impactful steps, such as planning meals in advance, buying only what is needed, and finding creative ways to use leftovers, individuals can contribute to minimizing food waste in Austria. Together, we can work towards a more sustainable and resource-efficient future while tackling the issue of food waste in our homes.
